Think of it like collecting rent: Instead of just buying and holding Tesla shares, this strategy makes money whether the stock goes up, down, or sideways.

Stage 1 (Selling Puts) — We say to the market: "I'll agree to buy 100 Tesla shares at $310 — but only if you pay me a fee upfront." The market pays us that fee (the premium). If Tesla stays above $310, the agreement expires and we keep the money, then repeat. If Tesla drops below that price, we get the shares — but we already got paid, so our real cost is lower.

Stage 2 (Selling Calls) — Now we own Tesla shares. We say: "I'll sell my shares at a higher price — but pay me a fee to lock that in." Again, we collect the fee. If Tesla rises above that price, we sell the shares at a profit. If it doesn't, we keep the fee and do it again.

The result: We collect income every 2–4 weeks regardless of direction. The only real risk is if Tesla drops severely and stays down for a long time.
